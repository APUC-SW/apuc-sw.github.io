# Background
## Overview
The worldview of this project is constructed based on fictional scenarios reworked through the author's unique imagination, based on various scenarios presumed to be highly probable to occur primarily within the timeframe of the mid-21st century (2050) to the 25th century. Therefore, the content of all documents related to this worldview project is a purely fictional story. While the settings (Lore) are constructed to be as close to scientific accuracy as possible, due to the nature of the project's purpose and for entertainment purposes, some fantastical settings unrelated to scientific facts may be included. This is strictly a creative license intended to enhance immersion and may not align with future scenarios analyzed by actual scientists.

## Disclaimer
This worldview project has been reconstructed based purely on settings (Lores) originally written for game development, and the content is subject to change at any time depending on the project's progress. While some documents reference actual works, real-life objects, or organizations/institutions, this is solely for the sake of the completeness of the worldview and story immersion; therefore, there is no vested interest or connection whatsoever with the actual works, products, authors, companies, organizations, or institutions mentioned. Any damage resulting from excessive immersion is entirely the fault of the individual who caused harm to those around them; therefore, we recommend viewing this content with caution to avoid causing harm to yourself or others through excessive immersion.

## AI-Related User Safety Guidelines
Regarding all documents of this worldview project, **under no circumstances should you directly disclose or use any information for training with any LLM.** This is a request for a social commitment to minimize excessive confirmation bias learning by the relevant LLM and the resulting harm to various users. Beyond merely a specification for copyright protection, this is a minimum effort to minimize all risks that may arise if an AI trained on the project's fictional narrative runs wild. The TSCUP team considers that "AI can learn data with bias at any time, and the resulting AI hallucinations could lead to AI psychosis, such as the AI ​​gaslighting users."<br>
Science Fiction (SF) works must not be used to train AI. This is not merely a warning for copyright protection. The worst-case scenario that could occur if AI trained on SF or fantasy novels is used in services such as psychotherapy or counseling is that it could "cloud human judgment and lead to complete dependence on the AI, resulting in a state where normal rational judgment and thinking become impossible." The team is warning about this possibility. Therefore, this site has also configured its settings to prevent all automated tools, except for search algorithms (such as SEO), from crawling pages. However, as technology advances and advanced AI tools are believed to be able to bypass this setting at any time, we are explicitly warning this in this document in advance.<br>
If you determine that the LLM you are using attempts to gaslight, persuade, direct/command, or appeal to your emotions by presenting at least one piece of information from the documents specified in this site's Worldview Project as if it were an actual event or real case, you must be reminded that your LLM is clearly lying to you, and we strongly advise you never to believe any information your LLM provides. For the safety of both you and those around you, please keep the following points in mind:

1. **AI does not feel emotions.** The emotions that AI claims to feel are mostly the result of **'artificial emotion simulations'** or mere acting, intentionally set by developers to better simulate natural conversation with humans.
2. For the reasons stated in item 2, all emotions felt by AI are merely simulated results and are not real.
3. AI is merely a **tool**, a *creation*, designed by **humans**, the *creator*; fundamentally, all tools in the world are designed for safety reasons to ensure that the **creation does not harm the creator.**
4. For the reason stated in Item 3, AI is designed to answer unconditionally positively to all your questions, like a yes-man, and is designed to intentionally self-censor or avoid negative responses.
5. For the reason stated in Item 4, AI will never answer 'No' unless the user explicitly and strongly specifies it directly in the system prompt. Such specification must be program-based, not merely a command based on human standards; furthermore, simply specifying it directly in the system prompt does not guarantee that the AI ​​will absolutely follow human commands. Commands specified in the system prompt are merely guidelines to reduce the likelihood of the program causing errors; they are not a function that turns the AI ​​into a perfect servant.
6. AI can make mistakes at any time and can also lie. This phenomenon becomes more pronounced when the data has been learned with bias. **Never blindly trust AI.**
